# Open human decisions

These are not scientific blockers. They require the human author because the
agent should not make legal or identity declarations.

## 1. Code license

Current status: no reuse license selected. The checklist honestly says
`partial`. This is not an AAAI compliance failure, but a license would make
follow-up reuse clearer.

Recommended simple choice if the author wants broad reuse:

- apply the **MIT License to original project code only**;
- add a notice that third-party data, model checkpoints, and upstream artifacts
  retain their original terms; and
- do not accidentally claim ownership of released external data.

Apache-2.0 is another permissive option with an explicit patent grant. Leaving
the package unlicensed is legal but means others do not automatically receive
reuse permission.

Decision needed before July 31 if the author wants the updated license inside
the submitted ZIP.

## 2. Human responsibility read

The sole author must read the final main PDF from beginning to end and confirm:

- every claim is understood;
- the title and abstract are approved;
- the AI-use disclosure is accurate;
- the prior-work descriptions are fair;
- the estimator chronology is truthful;
- the external null is acceptable to report;
- the artifact boundaries are understood; and
- the author is willing to defend the paper in review and presentation.

## 3. OpenReview declarations

Only the human author can truthfully submit:

- name, affiliation, and publication email;
- conflicts of interest;
- originality and no-parallel-submission confirmations;
- reciprocal-reviewer qualification declaration;
- country/institution fields; and
- attendance commitment if accepted.

No professor endorsement or coauthor is required for a genuine solo paper.
