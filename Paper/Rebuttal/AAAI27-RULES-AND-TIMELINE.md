# AAAI-27 rules and timeline

Verified against live official pages on **July 18, 2026**.

## Baby-food version

There are two review rounds.

1. **Phase 1:** two human reviews plus one clearly labeled AI-generated review.
   The AI review has no score and no formal decision power. A sufficiently
   negative paper can be rejected here. There is no rebuttal before that reject.
2. **Phase 2:** surviving papers receive additional independent human reviews,
   for up to five reviews total. Then the author gets one written feedback
   window from October 19 through October 25.

Humans make the decisions. The October response can answer every review,
including the AI-generated review.

## What reviewers score

The official main-track criteria are:

- significance and novelty;
- theoretical or empirical soundness;
- relevance to the AAAI community;
- clarity of exposition;
- responsible research practices; and
- steps supporting reproducibility.

AAAI explicitly allows empirical, integrative, and critical contributions. It
also says solid work that opens new territory or points to a new direction can
be preferable to a narrow incremental benchmark improvement.

## What the October response is for

AAAI describes author feedback as an opportunity to address reviewer questions
or factual mistakes. It is not a second paper deadline.

During feedback:

- the main PDF stays frozen;
- the supplement stays frozen;
- the code/data ZIP stays frozen;
- no new figure or replacement file can be uploaded; and
- the author submits written answers only.

No official AAAI-27 feedback word or character limit is public yet. The public
OpenReview rebuttal form has not been deployed. Do not invent a limit from an
older conference.

## Exact modification windows

| Time | What can still change | What is frozen |
|---|---|---|
| Now through July 21 | All fields and files | Nothing conference-frozen yet |
| After July 21 through July 28 | Author list/order and main PDF; only non-substantive title, TL;DR, and abstract edits | Topics and reciprocal-reviewer nomination |
| After July 28 through July 31 | Supplementary materials only | Main PDF, authors, title, TL;DR, abstract, and topics |
| After July 31 through decisions | Nothing except final withdrawal | Every submission field and file |
| October 19–25 | Written author feedback | PDFs, ZIPs, metadata, and artifacts |
| Camera-ready, if accepted | Final source/PDF, identities, acknowledgments, appropriate clarification; title-change request | Authors cannot be added or deleted |
| After December 14 | Publisher-requested changes only | Author-initiated changes |

AAAI says paper withdrawals are final.

## Paper and upload limits

- Seven pages of technical content.
- Maximum nine pages total.
- Pages after page seven may contain references only.
- Main PDF: 10 MB.
- Reproducibility checklist: 5 MB.
- Technical supplement PDF: 10 MB.
- Code/data ZIP: 50 MB.
- Media ZIP: 50 MB.
- The supplement is optional and reviewers are not required to read it.
- Essential evidence must therefore be in the main paper.

## Blind review

- No author names, affiliations, acknowledgments, personal URLs, or identifying
  repository links in the review files.
- The submission may cite public prior literature normally.
- A public unrefereed preprint is allowed, but the AAAI submission must not point
  to it and the public preprint must not advertise that it was submitted to
  AAAI-27.
- Supplementary files must also be anonymous.

## Generative AI

AAAI allows judicious author use of generative AI. The human author remains
responsible for every sentence, result, reference, figure, and artifact. AAAI's
publication policy requires the role of AI systems to be documented in the
manuscript. The current paper includes a specific `Use of AI Systems` paragraph
on page 7.

Do not try to “beat an AI detector.” The defensible strategy is an accurate
disclosure, verified references, reproducible numbers, candid limits, and a
human author who reads and owns the work.
