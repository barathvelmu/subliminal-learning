## Baby-food version

There are two versions of this package:

1. The small **Review Pack** is for reading and emailing to family. It omits the
   large code/data ZIP.
2. The **Complete Package** adds the code/data ZIP and contains all four files
   prepared for OpenReview.

In both versions, `Rebuttal-Shield/` helps the author understand and answer
reviewer criticism later. Those Markdown files are **not** July upload files.

Nothing in this package has been submitted automatically.

The page count is **7 pages of main paper + 3 pages of technical supplement +
2 pages of checklist**. The phrase `12 pages inspected` refers to those three
separate PDFs together, not a twelve-page main paper.

The final July 19 audit rebuilt all three PDFs, inspected every page, verified
anonymity and fonts, checked all 23 citations, passed all 49 archive-manifest
hashes, regenerated all included analyses, and reproduced all three figures
byte-for-byte. It added one directly relevant July 5 contemporaneous citation
and one notation clarification; a second scan found no further justified edit.

## The four submission files

- `01-Main-Paper.pdf`: the seven-page paper reviewers judge.
- `02-Reproducibility-Checklist.pdf`: the separate required checklist.
- `03-Technical-Supplement.pdf`: extra methods and robustness evidence.
- `04-Code-and-Data.zip`: anonymous saved data and analysis code. This appears
  only in the Complete Package because it is about 49.5 MB.

Do not unzip and re-zip `04-Code-and-Data.zip`. Upload it exactly as supplied.

## What the rebuttal shield means

AAAI first screens the paper in Phase 1. A paper rejected in Phase 1 does not
receive an author-response opportunity. A paper that reaches Phase 2 can answer
reviews during the October author-feedback window. That response explains and
points to evidence; it cannot replace the frozen July PDFs or ZIP.

The shield contains simulated skeptical reviews, a claim-to-evidence map,
careful draft answers, and a checklist for deciding which reviewer points are
correct. It is preparation, not a promise that the paper will be accepted.

## What the author must do

1. Read the complete main paper personally.
2. Read `Roadmap/START-HERE-SUBMISSION-ROADMAP.md`.
3. Truthfully complete the OpenReview identity, conflict, reviewer, and policy
   fields.
4. Upload the exact four frozen files in one sitting and download them back
   once to verify them. The three dates are deadlines, not three different
   submissions.

The code archive currently has no public reuse license. That is already
disclosed honestly in the checklist. Do not rebuild the audited ZIP tomorrow
just to add one; licensing can be decided deliberately later.

The paper is solo-authored. AAAI does not require a ceremonial coauthor,
professor sponsor, or endorsement for the conference submission.
