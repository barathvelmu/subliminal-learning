## The two websites are different

### 1. AAAI / OpenReview

This is the conference submission. You do **not** need a professor to endorse
you. You do need:

1. A complete OpenReview profile.
2. Your real author name and affiliation.
3. Every real coauthor added before the author deadline.
4. Conflicts of interest completed honestly.
5. Confirmation that the same work is not simultaneously under review at
   another archival venue.
6. Acceptance of the author/reviewer obligations unless AAAI grants an
   exemption.

The AAAI-27 deadlines are July 21, 2026 for the abstract, July 28 for the full
paper, and July 31 for supplement/code, all at 11:59 PM UTC-12. Official pages:

- https://aaai.org/conference/aaai/aaai-27/main-technical-track-call/
- https://aaai.org/conference/aaai/aaai-27/submission-instructions/

### 2. arXiv

arXiv is an optional public preprint. It is not the AAAI submission and it is
not required for AAAI. arXiv may ask for endorsement when you submit your first
paper or enter a new category.

The complete step-by-step instructions, category choice, license choice,
anonymity rules, and future-venue decision tree are in
[`arxiv-and-other-venues-guide.md`](arxiv-and-other-venues-guide.md).

Baby-food version: start an arXiv draft and choose the category. The website
will tell you whether endorsement is needed. An institutional email or claimed
coauthored paper can help. If it still asks, contact one active arXiv author who
knows the field, such as a professor, and send that person the endorsement link
arXiv gives you. Do not mass-email strangers.

Official explanation: https://info.arxiv.org/help/endorsement.html

## What a professor can help with

A professor is useful for a serious final read, scientific feedback, and an
arXiv endorsement if arXiv requests one. A professor should be a coauthor only
if they make a real scholarly contribution and accept responsibility for the
paper. Do not add someone only for status.
